**관련성 점수:** 8  

**본문:**  
SEOUL, Nov 9 (Reuters) - South Korean equities are poised to hit record highs this week, buoyed by a series of bullish investment recommendation reports from leading global brokerages.  
Analysts at Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley have raised their 12-month target for the benchmark Kospi index to 3,000 points, citing robust export figures and easing inflation pressures.  
“Our checks indicate that foreign investors are increasing their exposure, particularly in technology and auto sectors,” said Jamie Lee, Asia-Pacific economist at Goldman Sachs.  
The Kospi closed at 2,950.12 on Friday, just 1.7% below its all-time high of 3,000.05 registered in 2021.  
Meanwhile, the Kosdaq, which lists smaller tech and biotech companies, is expected to benefit from fresh coverage by US research firms.  
Nomura upgraded its outlook to “overweight,” highlighting semiconductor equipment makers as potential outperformers.  
Local brokerages are also optimistic, with Mirae Asset raising targets on several heavyweights, including Samsung Electronics and LG Chem.  
Investors will be eyeing advanced U.S. inflation data later this week for further cues on Fed policy.
